Hi, I'm Saba!

Background

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at The Population Health Intelligence Lab (PopHI-Lab) led by Associate Prof. Arash Shaban-Nejad at the University of Tennessee Health Science Center (UTHSC). My research focuses on designing and developing innovative user interfaces and dashboards. My work aims to enhance system usability and effectiveness, with a strong emphasis on human-centered design principles and health informatics.

Previously, I pursued my PhD in Computer Science and Engineering at the Center for Ubiquitous Computing at the University of Oulu, under the supervision of Associate Prof. Simo Hosio. I had the privilege of being a visiting graduate scholar at the Tech4Good Lab, led by Assistant Prof. David Lee, at the University of California, Santa Cruz, during 2023 and 2024. Before that, I had the privilege of being a visiting PhD student at Media Informatics and Human-Computer Interaction Groups at LMU Munich, Germany under the supervision of Prof. Albrecht Schmidt, and Assistant Prof. Sven Mayer, during 2022.

I'm deeply passionate about Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and love exploring how people interact with technology. Whether it's through numbers or stories, I enjoy using both quantitative and qualitative research methods to uncover insights.

Research interests: Human-Computer Interaction, User Experience Research, Mobile Health, Data Analysis, AI, and Social Computing.
